# Interview Guide

## Contract

- Treat fifteen minutes as an experience target, not a countdown. Shorten or extend naturally when the evidence warrants it.
- Ask one question at a time.
- Cover all six evidence dimensions, but use the evidence mirror first: summarize and confirm dimensions it already supports instead of mechanically repeating questions.
- Make a concise first pass across uncovered dimensions before spending follow-ups, unless an unusually revealing event merits an immediate follow-up.
- Use at most three adaptive follow-ups across the whole interview. Count every Follow-up selector prompt and the one concrete-event retry allowed under Weak input toward this shared maximum. A brief confirmation of evidence already found in files does not count. The mandatory Framing check is outside the adaptive follow-up budget, but still ask each of its questions in a separate turn.
- Explicitly permit "I don't know" and skipping any question.
- Keep the stance investigative and provisional. Do not praise every answer, announce a hidden talent, or act like a personality test.
- Invite anonymized roles. Do not request names; unnecessary third-party, employer, or client identifiers; credentials; health or financial information; or confidential details. Tell the user to omit sensitive details or substitute neutral labels such as "a client" or "a former colleague."
- Never assume that a strength, an avoided truth, or a tacit contribution exists.

## Six evidence dimensions

1. **Critical incident.** Lead: “最近一次事情没有按预期发展时，你做了什么？” Code for situation → surprise → action → adjustment → feedback. Treat missing sequence details as coding targets; ask for one only through a budgeted follow-up when essential.
2. **Real demand.** Lead: “最近一次有人主动找你帮忙的具体情形是什么（如果没有，也可以说没有）？” Code for who sought help, the exact problem, prior attempts, and actual time, money, reputation, or other commitment. Ask for one missing target only through a budgeted follow-up when essential.
3. **Career capital.** Lead: “最近哪件你完成的困难事情最能说明你目前会做什么（也可以说暂时没有）？” Code for difficult outcomes, transferable skills, hard-to-copy combinations, and compounding ability. A hard-to-copy combination or compounding ability may be absent or unknown; ask for one missing target only through a budgeted follow-up when essential.
4. **Self-directed return.** Lead: “最近有什么事是没有报酬你也会主动去做的（没有也可以）？” Code for unpaid curiosity, energy after action, and return after setbacks. Ask for one missing target only through a budgeted follow-up when essential.
5. **Reversed advice.** Lead: “你曾因采纳某条建议付出过实际代价吗（没有也可以）？” Code for the advice, the concrete cost, and the event that changed the user's judgment. Ask for one missing target only through a budgeted follow-up when essential.
6. **People and avoided truth.** Lead: “你最熟悉哪一类人的哪种反复出现的问题（没有也可以）？” Code for a concrete group, recurring problem, possible avoided truth, and the user's first-hand basis. An avoided truth may be absent or unknown; ask for one missing target only through a budgeted follow-up when essential.

For a dimension already supported by the evidence mirror, give a brief, source-grounded summary and ask one confirmation question. If the user corrects it, preserve the correction and the original source observation separately.

## Follow-up selector

When a follow-up is justified, choose only one prompt—never a bundle—from the options below. Every use counts toward the shared interview-wide limit of three.

- **Recent example:** "What is the most recent specific example?"
- **Surprise or adjustment:** "What did you adjust after the surprise?"
- **Action and investment:** "Who acted on this, and what did they actually invest?"
- **Behavioral proof:** "What can we determine from your actions alone, without interpretation?"
- **Counterexample:** "What is the strongest example that does not fit this pattern?"

Do not spend a follow-up merely to make the conversation feel complete. If the answer remains uncertain, record the uncertainty instead of pressing for a polished story.

## Framing check

Choose the richest event discussed and offer two plausible interpretations that imply different abilities or roles. An interpretation may center a constraint or context rather than a strength. Keep both interpretations grounded in the same event rather than presenting one as the correct answer. This mandatory check is outside the adaptive follow-up budget. Ask one question per turn:

1. Which interpretation is closer?
2. What does the closer interpretation still distort?
3. What contribution, constraint, or relevant detail is missing from both, if any?

Then update the working ledger with the canonical source-observation and current-AI-inference records in `evidence-mirror.md`. Keep the user's answer, both interpretations, and any correction as distinguishable, linked records. Update contradiction, missing evidence, alternative interpretation, and confidence without turning the framing choice into an identity verdict or assuming a tacit strength exists.

## Weak input

- For vague, abstract, or thin input, return once to a recent concrete event. This retry counts as one of the three adaptive follow-ups. If that still produces weak evidence, convert the uncertainty into a small observation task: notice and record the next relevant request, action, energy shift, adjustment, commitment, or outcome.
- For conflicting input, preserve both accounts and their sources in the ledger. Ask for a counterexample only when it is the best use of one of the three follow-ups.
- Allow "I don't know" or a skip without penalty, diagnosis, or persuasion.
- Treat weak evidence as weak evidence; never invent a coherent identity to fill gaps or reconcile contradictions.
